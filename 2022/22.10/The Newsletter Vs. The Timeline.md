# The Newsletter Vs. The Timeline

![Newsletters are taking over](_mailicon.png)

I've noticed my RSS feeds have been drying up lately. It used to be that Reeder was the first place I went to refill my cup when I was thirsty for knowledge and entertainment. People used to complain that they couldn't keep up with their RSS feeds. There were articles about quitting RSS after coming back from vacation to pile of unread posts. I understood the sentiment. At times, my only defense against the overwhelm of items was a "mark all as read" button prematurely pressed. 

Recently, I've stopped checking my feed reader so much because there just isn't that much in there. A lot of writers have turned to newsletters (and, more specifically, Substack). Using a reader like Feedbin allows me to ingest those newsletters, but the formatting isn't great and you can't reply to the author (one of my favorite features of newsletters).
