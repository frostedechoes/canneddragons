# Is Live Music Dying?

[This article by Nate Rogers](https://www.theringer.com/music/2022/12/21/23519063/taylor-swift-ticketmaster-tours-canceled-live-nation-concert-industry) for *The Ringer* centers around what Ticketmaster has done to the live music industry, but branches out into some broader topics. He spends a fair amount of time on poptimism and the problems that have come from the changes in some of the dominant music paradigms that came with the movement. 

> “Income inequality is obviously a society-wide problem,” said Hyden. “But when it comes to art, I don’t feel like the people with the most money make the best music. I think that’s been disproven over and over again. And I don’t think that’s true now. A lot of the big pop records, I think, are boring as hell.”

I felt like Rogers could have expanded on the role that Spotify has played in some of the problematic shifts in music in general, but was surprised to learn that *The Ringer* is owned by Spotify.
