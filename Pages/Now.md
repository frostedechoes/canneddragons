# Now

*Inspired by* *[Derek Silvers](https://sivers.org/nowff)*, *this page includes a sample of what I’m thinking about and working on right now. Last updated December 29, 2022.*

---

While I’m on that interstitial time between Christmas and New Year’s, I thought I would update my Now page, which I haven’t done since the summer (bad on me). I’ve continued on a good trajectory, health-wise. I only had to use one PTO day for not feeling well this year, which is wonderful considering the illness of the last few years and how many days I was using. This break for the holidays has been welcome, though. Both of my sisters were in town and staying with my mom, which is a rare treat. We have been beset by some seriously cold weather, but it has eased up some, and we’ve been able to get out for walks. Helena Davis [wrote about this "dead week"](https://www.theatlantic.com/family/archive/2021/12/christmas-new-years-dead-week-romjul/621098/) for *The Atlantic*. 

> Even so, it is still the closest thing we have in our society to some kind of a communal pause. Nothing is ever as quiet as it is during those few days, cities emptied out and small towns sleepier than usual, people drifting around not interested in accomplishing anything. There is a collective sense that, for these few days, we are not going to do any more than we must. It doesn’t really matter if you don’t brush your hair, if you stay up all night, if you don’t send that work email. Many people aren’t checking email anyway, and nobody wants to be asked to do more work than they absolutely have to.

My youngest son turns on the *Tiny House Nation* channel in the bedroom before we go to sleep at night. That means constantly refreshing my fascination with tiny homes. Though there are too many limitations for our family to live in one, I get fixated on these efficient and beautiful abodes. I start thinking about where we could live if we owned a tiny home. I start mapping out logistics. Overall, I guess there are worse ways to spend your time.[^1]

After a year of experimentation, I'm finally getting settled on a consistent blogging workflow, which I'll inevitably write about at some point (hint – blogging with git on mobile isn't as hard as you might think). My main blog is now [canneddragons.net](https://canneddragons.net). It doesn't have a newsletter, so I will kind of miss that avenue for communication, but I'm happy with it otherwise. Maybe I'll be able to get my mom into RSS.[^2]

I'm watching _Andor_ and getting close to the end. This is the darkest Star Wars narrative I've seen. It takes the evil that was somewhat abstract and makes it more personal, intensely visceral and very concrete. No longer are we given a shot of a planet blowing up, only to have to use our imaginations stretching capabilities to picture what the actual impact of the act is. Now we are shown, in microscopic detail, just what effect the empire has on the galaxy and its peoples. It can be tedious, but at the same time, it's eye-opening.

From a spiritual perspective, as I pursue the richness of the Orthodox Christian tradition, I am finding its depth. I'm enjoying plumbing those depths with books such as _The Religion of the Apostles_ by Stephen DeYoung. I also found [this video](https://youtu.be/KX4zpZE-Yqk) to be incredibly instructive, as a Protestant learning about the Orthodox faith. 

It is my hope that you all are having a wonderful holiday season and getting some kind of break this time of year. Thanks for reading Canned Dragons!
